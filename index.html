Problem Statement

Smallholder farmers produce 33% of the world's food on farms smaller than 2 hectares, yet 50% of their harvests never reach market due to preventable losses. In Kenya specifically, where agriculture employs 40% of the population and contributes 26% of GDP, smallholder farmers face a devastating information gap: they lack real-time soil intelligence to optimise inputs, have no early-warning system for crop disease, and are shut out of fair market prices by information asymmetry.

Farmers sell produce at 40–50% below market value to middlemen, while post-harvest losses consume 45% of total fruit and vegetable production. Less than 10% of farmers have access to market price data, and rural internet penetration stands at just 34% versus 85% urban.
